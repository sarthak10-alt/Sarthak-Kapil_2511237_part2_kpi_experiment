# Recommendation Memo

## 1. Executive Summary
The treatment onboarding campaign should be launched with monitoring. The treatment group achieved a higher paid conversion rate than the control group, and the improvement was statistically significant.

## 2. North Star Metric
The North Star metric selected is **paid conversion rate**.

Control paid conversion rate: 3.19%  
Treatment paid conversion rate: 7.04%  
Absolute lift: 3.85%  
Relative lift: 120.87%

## 3. KPI Tree Explanation
The KPI tree links the North Star metric to three primary driver areas:
1. Activation quality
2. Revenue quality
3. User experience guardrails

The purpose is to ensure the campaign is not judged only by conversion improvement, but also by revenue quality and user experience risks.

## 4. Experiment Result Summary
Treatment performed better on the main conversion metric.

Key results:
- Landing page visit rate improved.
- Trial start rate improved.
- Onboarding completion rate improved.
- Paid conversion rate improved.
- Engagement score improved.
- Days to convert decreased.

## 5. Hypothesis Test Interpretation
A one-tailed two-proportion z-test was used.

P-value: 0.000549

Since p-value < 0.05, the result is statistically significant. This means the observed improvement is unlikely to be due to random chance.

## 6. Guardrail Analysis
The treatment has some guardrail risks:
- Support ticket rate increased.
- Refund requests appeared in the treatment group.
- Average revenue per converted user decreased.

Positive guardrails:
- Engagement score increased.
- Days to convert decreased.

## 7. Segment-Level Insight
The treatment showed strong improvement in most major segments. Referral, Paid Search, Email, and Organic traffic improved. Social traffic declined slightly and should be monitored.

## 8. Final Recommendation
**Launch the treatment experience.**

The recommendation is to launch because the North Star metric improved significantly and the statistical test supports the result.

## 9. Risks and Limitations
- Support load may increase after launch.
- Refund behavior should be monitored.
- Social traffic performance needs attention.
- Revenue per converted user was lower in Treatment, so revenue quality should be tracked after launch.

## 10. Next Steps
1. Launch the treatment experience to all users.
2. Monitor paid conversion rate weekly.
3. Track refund rate and support ticket rate.
4. Investigate Social traffic performance.
5. Recheck revenue quality after launch.
